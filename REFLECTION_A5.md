This assignment required translating system requirements into structured use cases and test cases. One of the main challenges was ensuring that all requirements identified in Assignment 4 were accurately represented in system interactions.

A significant challenge was structuring use cases to clearly reflect user actions without redundancy. It was important to ensure that each use case represented a specific system function while also capturing dependencies such as checking availability before booking appointments.

Another challenge was writing detailed specifications. Defining preconditions and postconditions required a clear understanding of system behavior. Additionally, identifying alternative flows ensured that potential errors, such as missing inputs or unavailable time slots, were properly handled.

Developing test cases required careful alignment with functional requirements. Each test case needed to clearly define steps, expected outcomes, and validation criteria. Non-functional testing introduced additional complexity, particularly in considering performance under high load and ensuring system security.

Maintaining consistency across all assignments was also critical. The stakeholders, system requirements, and system behavior needed to align across Assignments 3, 4, and 5. This required careful review and refinement of previous work.

Overall, this assignment improved my understanding of how system requirements are transformed into practical models and validation mechanisms. It emphasized the importance of clear documentation, structured design, and thorough testing in software engineering.

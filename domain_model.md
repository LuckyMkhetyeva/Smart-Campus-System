Domain Model – Smart Campus Attendance and Appointment System
Overview

The domain model represents the core entities of the Smart Campus System, including their attributes, responsibilities, relationships, and business rules. It provides a conceptual understanding of how different parts of the system interact.

Entity Definitions

Entity	Attributes	Methods / Responsibilities	Relationships
Student	studentId, fullName, email	bookAppointment(), viewAttendance()	Books multiple Appointments (1 to many); Has multiple Attendance records (1 to many)
Lecturer	lecturerId, fullName, department	approveAppointment(), recordAttendance()	Manages multiple Appointments (1 to many); Records multiple Attendance records (1 to many)
Appointment	appointmentId, date, status	approve(), reject(), cancel()	Linked to one Student (many to one); Linked to one Lecturer (many to one)
Attendance	attendanceId, date, status	markAttendance(), updateAttendance()	Linked to one Student (many to one); Recorded by one Lecturer (many to one)
Admin	adminId, fullName	manageUsers(), generateReports()	Manages Students and Lecturers; Generates multiple Reports (1 to many)
Report	reportId, reportType, generatedDate	generate(), view()	Generated by one Admin (many to one)
Notification	notificationId, message, status	sendNotification()	Sent to Students and Lecturers (many to many)


Relationships Description
A Student can book multiple appointments, but each appointment belongs to one student.
A Lecturer can manage multiple appointments and is responsible for approving or rejecting them.
Attendance records are linked to students and can only be created or modified by lecturers.
An Admin manages system users and generates reports for monitoring and decision-making.
Notifications are used to communicate system updates to both students and lecturers.

Business Rules
A student cannot book overlapping appointments.
An appointment must be approved by a lecturer before it is confirmed.
Attendance can only be recorded and updated by lecturers.
Reports can only be generated by administrators.
Notifications must be sent when appointment status changes (approved or rejected).

Conclusion

The domain model provides a structured and well-defined representation of the Smart Campus System. It ensures consistency with system requirements, supports clear understanding of entity relationships, and forms a strong foundation for the class diagram and system implementation.

Smart Campus Attendance and Appointment System

Overview

The Smart Campus Attendance and Appointment System aims to improve the management of attendance records and academic appointment scheduling within educational institutions. The system supports various stakeholders who interact with the platform in different ways. Each stakeholder has specific concerns, challenges, and expectations regarding how the system should function.

Students

Role:
Students use the system to book appointments with lecturers and view their attendance records.

Key Concerns:
Students want a system that is easy to use and allows quick access to appointment booking and attendance information.

Pain Points:
Students often struggle to schedule consultations with lecturers due to limited communication channels and unclear availability.

Success Metrics:
Students should be able to successfully book an appointment within one minute and easily access their attendance information.

Lecturers

Role:
Lecturers are responsible for recording student attendance and managing appointment requests from students.

Key Concerns:
Lecturers require an efficient and accurate system to record attendance and manage student consultations without administrative burden.

Pain Points:
Traditional attendance tracking methods such as paper registers can lead to errors, lost records, and scheduling conflicts.

Success Metrics:
Lecturers should be able to record attendance within ten seconds and easily manage appointment approvals.

System Administrator

Role:
The system administrator manages the system, ensures its proper functioning, and monitors overall usage.

Key Concerns:
Administrators require reliable system performance, proper user access control, and efficient monitoring tools.

Pain Points:
Managing multiple student records and system users manually can be complex and time-consuming.

Success Metrics:
The system should maintain uptime above 99% and allow administrators to manage users efficiently.

University Management

Role:
University management oversees academic operations and uses system data to evaluate institutional performance.

Key Concerns:
Management requires accurate reports and insights about attendance and academic engagement.

Pain Points:
Without a centralized system, collecting attendance data and generating reports is difficult and inefficient.

Success Metrics:
The system should allow management to generate reports within five seconds.

IT Support Staff

Role:
IT support staff maintain the system infrastructure and ensure the platform runs smoothly.

Key Concerns:
They require a system that is easy to maintain, update, and troubleshoot.

Pain Points:
Systems with poor documentation and complex architecture are difficult to maintain and upgrade.

Success Metrics:
System updates should be deployed within one hour with minimal disruption.

Academic Advisors

Role:
Academic advisors monitor student performance and engagement, including attendance patterns.

Key Concerns:
Advisors need reliable access to attendance data to support students effectively.

Pain Points:
Without proper systems, advisors lack visibility into student participation and engagement.

Success Metrics:
Advisors should be able to instantly view attendance statistics and identify students who may need academic support.
